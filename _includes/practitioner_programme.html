Bates Vision Education Practitioner Programme

Eyesight

Eyesight is a learned process which depends on internal reflexes and external stimuli. It depends on a weaving of inner and outer experiences.

Eyesight needs the electromagnetic energy of light from the sun and physical, mental, emotional, social and spiritual awareness. A dynamic relating of all these elements permeates healthy vision.

In the Embodied Vision Bates Method Practitioner Programme we explore and engage with all these aspects of vision through supported experiential study.

5 day in-person modules

Each module will weave in the essential elements of light, rest, movement, memory and imagination. Through practical sessions students will work with each other and as a group to learn how to teach Bates Method Vision Education.

The modules also provide

  • a supportive space to explore your own eyesight
  • professional awareness of vision and Vision Education practices
  • clinical experience
  • in-person experience for teachers who have learned online

The modules can be taken separately but also layer together, in any order, to form a complete professional training with over 200 hours of contact time. Students who successfully complete all six modules and the homework requirements will become certified Bates Vision Education teachers.

Programme Director and Teacher - Anna Bambridge

Anna first encountered the potential of natural eyesight work in 1996 and has been immersed in her own vision journey ever since.

She graduated from the College of Vision Education as a professional Bates Vision teacher in 2006. Her teachers there were Aileen Whiteford and Margaret Montgomery, part of a teaching lineage that is unbroken from W.H. Bates.

Anna has continually developed her vision work and has deepened her understanding through workshops and individual lessons in-person with Anthony Attenborough, Brigitte Cavadias, Peter Grunwald, Nina Hutchings, Roberto Kaplan, Fernanda Leite Ribeiro, Peter Mansfield, Mikolaj Markiewicz, Uschi Ostermeier, Meir Schneider and Esther van der Werf.

She has over 500 hours in-person one to one teaching experience as well as having taught many group workshops including at the International Holistic Vision Conference.

Online she has presented workshops for Redefine Vision and the Association of Vision Educators. She has been on panels with Gloria Ginn, Jacob Liberman, Daniela Matuchova, Claudia Muehlenweg and Nathan Oxenfeld. She has been a guest on the Better Eyesight and Naked Eye podcasts.

Anna studied physics at Cambridge University and has an MPhil in Vision Sciences. She will graduate as a Body-Mind Centering® Somatic Movement Educator lin June 2026. She is a member of the Bates Association for Vision Education, the Association of Vision Educators and the Body-Mind Centering® Association.

Teacher - Daniela Maťuchová

Daniela wore glasses for nearly twenty years. When she was on maternity leave with her first child she discarded the glasses and after some time she realized that her eyesight improved spontaneously. So, she found out that vision can improve.

Her second child was born prematurely and as a result suffers from cerebral palsy and is visually impaired. Daniela looked for any possibilities to help him and she found the Bates Method. She studied in London and graduated from the Bates College of Vision Education in 2013.

In 2014, she started her own business in Banská Bystrica, Slovakia. Daniela continues educating herself in the field of natural vision improvement and has attended workshops with Meir Schneider, Fernanda Leite Ribeiro, Mikolaj Markiewicz, Dror Schneider and many others. She has over 3000 hours in-person one to one teaching experience as well as having taught many group workshops and talks including one at the International Holistic Vision Conference in Edinburgh in 2017. She has been a guest on podcasts in Slovak Republic and Czech Republic and presented The Bates method on Slovak Television. She has written 3 books, one of them is in English – The Miracle of Sight and she contributed to the book A New World of Seeing. She has been a member of the Bates Association for Vision Education since 2013.

Teacher - Mary Flynn

Mary is a Bates Method teacher and Ophthalmological Nurse with 25 years experience in hospital eye and vision care.

She studied with Peter Mansfield and Margaret Montgomery at the School of Vision Education and qualified as a Bates teacher in 1995.

In 2017 she completed the 5 day Original Bates Method for Vision Educators taining with Esther Joy van der Werf and has had personal lessons or attended workshops with Gloria Ginn, Nina Hutchings, Dr Jerriann Taber, Maurizio Cagnoli and Aileen Whiteford. Mary is a member of the Bates Association for Vision Education.

"I grew up on a Farm in Co Cork, Ireland, where I enjoyed the outdoor life. I have been interested in eyesight since my early teens when I was prescribed my first glasses. I disliked wearing them but felt I had no choice when my sight was blurry for the school board.

I discovered the Bates Method through a book written by Peter Mansfield. I had always been interested in natural health and was fascinated that sight could improve. My vision story was that I experienced short-sight. I thoroughly enjoyed the process of exploration in learning the Bates Method. I loved looking at the world in new ways and learning what helped my vision.

I’m passionate about empowering and educating people about other possibilities regarding their health — in particular Eyecare and Vision."

Teacher - Jane Reed

Jane attended the College of Vision Education with teachers Aileen Whiteford and Margaret Montgomery and qualified as a Bates Method teacher in 2007.

"I am passionate about using my experience and knowledge to support people in discovering and nurturing their natural vision. I have worked with many clients over the years and it always brings me joy to share in their journey of self-discovery and clarity.

I first began wearing glasses at the age of 18, after leaving school and starting work. Prior to this, I had enjoyed perfect eyesight. After 20 years of lenses/glasses, my eyesight had steadily been deteriorating and my prescription escalated to +6.5 dioptres. It was at this point that I decided to look into the possibility of improving my vision by natural methods. I read “Better Eyesight Without Glasses” by W. H. Bates. Though difficult to understand, I followed the instructions written in the book and began to notice improvements in my vision. I was thrilled as I had never thought this was possible.

Over time I learned to make friends with the blur if it presented itself. I realised that the blur alerted me when I needed to take a more relaxed approach towards seeing rather than fighting and trying to force better vision.

I am so grateful that I began this vision journey. I have learned so much about how the way I choose to see the world affects my vision."

Jane is a member of the Bates Association for Vision Education.
